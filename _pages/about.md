I am a doctoral researcher at the **[Software Analysis and Intelligence Laboratory](https://www.wichita.edu/)**, Wichita State University, advised by Prof. Zhen Shan. My research sits at the intersection of **AI, program analysis, and mobile software security**.

I build research systems that combine trustworthy datasets, static analysis tooling, and LLM-assisted workflows to detect and reason about defects in Android applications. My current work focuses on three connected problems:

- **Android memory leak detection** — constructing a large-scale, manually validated benchmark of 650+ Android projects using the [Soot](https://soot-oss.github.io/soot/) static analysis framework.
- **LLM-assisted program analysis** — studying how large language models can triage, explain, and reason about complex Android bug patterns to assist developers and analysts.
- **Trustworthy security research** — advancing reproducibility and empirical rigor through principled dataset construction and credible evaluation frameworks.

Before my PhD, I spent three years in industry cybersecurity — as a **Senior Consultant at Ernst & Young** (Technology Risk, cybersecurity audits for PTCL and Ufone) and an **Information Systems Auditor at The Bank of Punjab** (security evaluation across 100+ banking applications). This background grounds my research in real-world security constraints and practitioner needs.

---

#### Research Themes

**LLMs for Program Analysis** &nbsp; I study how language model outputs can augment static analysis results, enabling defect explanation and reasoning at a scale and depth not achievable by traditional tooling alone.

**Android Static Analysis** &nbsp; I develop and validate Soot-based tooling for Android Java analysis, with a focus on memory leak patterns, benchmark construction, and empirical evaluation.

**Trustworthy Security Research** &nbsp; I design evaluation frameworks that prioritize manual validation and reproducibility, ensuring that published benchmarks reflect real-world bug distributions rather than synthetic artifacts.
